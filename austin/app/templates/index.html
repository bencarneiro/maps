DANCE TEAM

What is Debut Performance Company?

Debut Performance Company is the 620 Dance Centre's non-competitive performance company.

Debut is designed for the dancer who is interested in building performance skills and improving technique. Students do not audition for a position on this team and are required to take a minimum of 2 different genres of dance in addition to weekly rehearsal.

Debut's goal is to provide dancers with a well-rounded professional program placing emphasis on developing the dancers's love for performance while building a strong foundation of technique.

Dancers participate in public performances in the local community and surrounding areas throughout the year. Dancers are also eligible to attend dance convention and workshop experiences with some of the most talented and sought after teachers in the United States. In doing so, dancers develop a strong technical background and grow to be confident performers.

Team members range in age from 7-18.

For more information, call our office at (512) 266-8888 -- we love to share about our wonderful dance team opportunity!

DRESS CODE

Dress Code

  • Proper dance attire is extremely important. A large percentage of dance is based on correct body placement.
  • Teachers need to be able to see a student’s body in order to give necessary corrections.
  • Accessories (t-shirts, shorts, sweats) are not acceptable for class attire. The teacher may ask at any time for these accessories to be removed.
  • Hair must be pulled back in a ponytail or a bun for class. If your child's hair is too short, please pull back with clips or a headband.

Children's Combo

A dance class designed to introduce young dancers to multiple forms of dance. Students will participate in age-appropriate ballet, tap and tumbling as they develop a love of dance.

  • Preschool Combo: A dance class of tap, ballet and tumbling for ages 3-4 with little or no dance experience. This class is focused on building developmental skills and balanced coordination for the youngest students while inspiring the love of dance.
  • Combo: A dance class of tap, ballet and tumbling for ages 4-5 with little or now dance experience. This class is focused on building strong skills and balanced coordination for elementary aged students while inspiring the love of dance.
  • Advanced Combo: A dance class of tap, ballet and pre-jazz for ages 6-7 with at least 2 years dance experience. This class is focused on continued skill building in both tap and ballet while offering an introduction to jazz. This class is appropriate for students moving up from Combo class.

Ballet

A classical dance form characterized by grace and precision of movement and by elaborate formal gestures, steps, and poses.

  • Ballet Beginner 1-A: ballet class for student age 7 and up with little or no prior dance experience. This class offers an introduction to ballet including barre and center work for young ballerinas. This class is appropriate for students moving up from Advanced Combo class or with teacher approval.
  • Ballet Beginner 2-A: ballet class for student age 8 and up with at least 1-2 years of ballet experience. This class is appropriate for students moving up from Beginner 1 or with teacher approval.
  • Ballet Advanced Beginner 1-A: ballet class for students ages 9 and up with at least 2 years of ballet training. This class is appropriate for students moving up from Beginner 2 or with teacher approval.
  • Ballet Intermediate 1-A: ballet class for students ages 11 and up with at least 4 years of ballet training. This class is appropriate for students moving up from Advanced Beginner 2 or with teacher approval.
  • Ballet Intermediate 2-A: ballet class for students ages 11 and up with at least 4-5 years of ballet training. This class is appropriate for students moving up from Advanced Intermediate or with teacher approval.
  • Ballet Advanced Intermediate 1-A: ballet class for students ages 12 and up with at least 6 years of ballet training. This class is appropriate for students moving up from Intermediate 2 or with teacher recommendation
  • Ballet Advanced Intermediate 2-A: ballet class for students ages 14 and up with 7-8 years of ballet training. This class is appropriate for students moving up from Advanced Intermediate 1 or with teacher recommendation.
  • Ballet Technique Intermediate/Advanced: This is a technique only class designed for Intermediate to Advanced ballet dancers who wish to gain strength and technical skill. Students must have a minimum of 6 years ballet training or instructor approval to attend this class. This class will not participate in the Spring Concert.
  • Ballet Teen Beginner: a ballet class specifically created for dancers ages 11 and up with prior experience as a young child or public school instruction. Class begins with basic barre work and terminology and progresses quickly into more advanced stages of ballet.
  • A ballet class specifically created for dancers ages 12 and up with prior studio experience or some public school instruction. Class begins with intermediate ballet techinques and terminology and progresses quickly into more advanced stages of technique and choreography. This call is recommended for students moving up from Ballet Teen Beginner.

Pointe

An extension of Ballet class for the Intermediate to Advanced student

  • Pre-Pointe: A preparatory pointe class designed to prepare the experienced ballet student for work in specially fitted pointe shoes. Dancers must be enrolled in an Advanced Intermediate Ballet class and have teacher recommendation.
  • Pointe 1-4: A pointe class designed to further a dancer’s ballet experience with level appropriate instruction, building both strength & technique. Dancers must be enrolled in upper level ballet classe and have teacher recommendation.

Contemporary

A dance form consisting of jazz, ballet & modern movements. Improvisation and musicality are explored through movement.

  • Contemporary Advanced Beginner-A dance class for students ages 9 and up. This class introduces dancers to the techniques of modern and contemporary dance with warm-up and center work. It is recommended that students have at least 2 years of ballet and jazz training. This is appropriate for students moving up from Beginner 2 and Advanced Beginner levels of Ballet & Jazz.
  • Contemporary Intermediate-A dance class for students ages 10 and up. This class consists of warm up and center work focused on modern and contemporary techniques. It is recommended that students have at least 4 years of ballet and jazz training (or teacher approval). This is appropriate for students moving up from Advanced Beginner or Intermediate levels of Ballet & Jazz.
  • Contemporary Teen- a dance class for students ages 11 and up. This class consists of warm up and center work focused on modern and contemporary techniques. It is recommended that students have at least 3 years of ballet and jazz training (or teacher approval). This is appropriate for students moving up from Modern 1, Advanced Beginner 2 and Intermediate levels of Ballet & Jazz.
  • Contemporary Advanced Intermediate-A dance class for students ages 13 and up. This class consists of warm up and center work focused on modern and contemporary techniques. It is recommended that students have at least 5 years of ballet and jazz training (or teacher approval). This is appropriate for students moving up from Intermediate levels of Ballet & Jazz.

Drill Team

Elements needed to participate in a public school dance team, pom squad, cheer team.

  • Drill Team Skills: This class is designed for students 6th grade and up who are interested in building skills in preparation for Middle School or High School dance team tryouts. Students will work on mastering required turns, leaps and kicks, as well as various dance styles, necessary for dance, drill & pom tryouts in area schools.

Hip Hop

A contemporary style of urban street dance. Hip Hop Dancers typically wear tennis shoes or sneakers and jeans, shorts or pants that are easy to move in. This is the newest form of dance and a super popular choice for kids, teens and adults!
